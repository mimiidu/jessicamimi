Assembly Tool

BRIEF

Design an assembly tool for a difficult-to-insert battery contact spring.

DETAILS

Role: Mechanical designer, Design verification, Manufacturing liaison
Tools: SolidWorks, Hand calculations, Testing
Manager: Kevin Eng, Kieran Corr (ecobee)

BACKGROUND

During manufacturing, assemblers were having difficulty inserting a battery contact spring clip into a plastic housing. My task was to design a tool to be used by the assemblers to streamline this process and minimize cycle time.

DESIGN

Different items were used for form inspiration of the tool. Two different designs of the fork were ultimately selected to be tested. Both designs were created in SolidWorks to be 3D printed and tested for usability, comfort, and cycle time. Tests were run with three different hand sizes- small, medium, and large. It was found that the long tool was more comfortable and easy to use, due to the thinner prongs and the longer handle. Cycle times were similar for both tools, with the wider tool having a slightly longer cycle time. From testing, it was clear that the longer tool was superior in comfort, speed, and usability and was selected for the final design. To suit larger hand sizes, the handle of the tool was slightly elongated.Â 

MANUFACTURING

To add additional comfort, the design was rounded all over, with a variable filet on the long edge. Aluminum was selected as the material after hand calculations to verify the prongs would not fail after prolonged use. Drawings were created using GD&T and the tool was manufactured through CNC, sandblasted and clear anodized to provide a comfortable feel in the hand and durable finish.

RESULT

The multiple copies of the tool were manufactured and employed in the factory, allowing assemblers to perform the battery contact spring insertion with ease.
