Packaging Design

BRIEF

Design packaging for existing products using biodegradable tray materials.

DETAILS

Role: Mechanical designer, Prototyper
Tools: SolidWorks, Hand calculations, Cardboard
Supervisor: Kevin Eng, Kieran Corr (ecobee)

BACKGROUND

A new product set including a camera and room sensor was being packaged to be sold. As this combination of products had not been sold before, new packaging needed to be designed. 

PROCESS

I sketched out a few different possible layouts for the packaging, and decided to reuse a square box design which was already in use for other products. I used an origami-style folded cardboard to hold the room sensors, designed to fit perfectly with an existing box, camera tray, accessories box and pamphlets to minimize new design and manufacturing. Instead of plastic trays, biodegradable injection molded trays made of different starches. The design was verified to ensured no damage would occur to camera lens and room sensors during shipping.

PET TRAYS

BRIEF

Design PET trays for a hero product to eliminate waste of 10,000 trays per year in the factory.

DETAILS

Role: Mechanical designer, Prototyper, Manufacturing liaison
Tools: SolidWorks
Supervisor: Kevin Eng, Kieran Corr (ecobee)

PROCESS

I identified a redundant use of trays in a factory- 10,000 trays were being thrown out per year due to an incongruence between the CM and the FATP factories. After communicating with both factories and learning the constraints, I designed a tray that would fit both factories to reduce plastic waste, increase the number of products shipped per skid, and save money on manufacturing trays. Factors considered during the design were box size, shipping, material selection and transportation. They were designed to be vacuum formed, keeping in mind the draft angle of 3 degrees.
