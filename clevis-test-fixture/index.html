Clevis Test Fixture

BRIEF

Design a test fixture to test the stitch strength of the payload webbing.

DETAILS

Role: Mechanical designer, Design verification, Manufacturing liaison
Tools: SolidWorks, Hand calculations
Mentor: Deepthi Krovvidi (Bumblebee Spaces)

BACKGROUND

Bumblebee Spaces is a ceiling-mounted cluster of furniture containing a queen sized bed and storage equivalent to a full closet. It aims to tackle the problem of over-population in dense urban areas by utilizing wasted vertical space. The furniture is raised and lowered through the use of kevlar-like webbing looped through a clevis joint.

DESIGN AND CONSTRAINTS

To test theÂ limits of the webbing stitch strength and simulate the cycling of the payloads, I designed a clevis fixture to designed to withstand 7000N of force with repeated cycling.

Hand calculations were used to verify shear, bending moment, or fatigue failures would not occur. The pin is manufactured of 1045H steel to ensure a FOS of 2, designed under the fatigue limit for infinite life. Mild steel was used for the clevis body.

OUTCOME

SolidWorks was used to design the assembly, and manufacturing was done through CNC and lathe, outsourced to a rapid prototyper. The parts were manufactured and implemented in testing.
