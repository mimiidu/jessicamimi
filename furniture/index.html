Platform Couch

BRIEF

Design a couch to seat at least 3 people for under $600 CAD.

DETAILS

Role: Mechanical designer, Construction, Sewing
Tools: SolidWorks, Hand calculations, Power tools, Sewing machine

BACKGROUND

I was looking to buy a couch for my parentsâ€™ living room, but I could not find a couch that fit the style and budget we were looking for. After some research, I decided to make my own as I could customize it to meet the requirements and still have it fit the budget.

OUTCOME

I sketched out the rough dimensions and proposed design then modelled the couch on SolidWorks, allowing me to quickly make a cut list for the pine frame.Â I ensured the screws and wood would not fail under normal usage through hand calculations before finalizing the design. I assembled the frame using dowels, screws, dove-tail joints, and stainless steel leg brackets before sanding and staining it. I rounded the corners for aesthetic and safety reasons with a disc sander. I bought a foam pad, cut it to size and used spray-adhesive to attach the batting. I sewed an inner lining for the cushion and sewed linen pillow covers andÂ cushion coverÂ in the pattern shown above. This pattern allows for the cover to be adjustable and remain tight overtime as linen can stretch or shrink based on usage. Overall, the project cost $400 CAD, very well within the budget.

KITCHEN BENCH

BRIEF

Redesign kitchen layout to have more storage and comfortably seat at least 4 people for under 5000 SEK.

DETAILS

Role: Mechanical designer, construction
Tools: SolidWorks, Hand calculations, Power tools, Sewing machine

BACKGROUND

My kitchen in Stockholm had an ill-fitting Ikea PAX shelf and a cramped dining space. It needed a redesign to better utilize the space. I considered three designs, including a coffee bar, a dining nook, and storage shelves. I ideally wanted to keep the budget below 5000 SEK, which is about $500 USD.

DETAILS

I decided on a dining nook as this arrangementÂ optimized the floor plan, made seating more comfortable, and allowed for a better view of the city through the window. I ensured the shelves and bench would not deflect more than 2.5 mm per meter through hand calculations, and attached the bench to the wall via anchors which could support the weight of three people with a safety factor of 2 (and thatâ€™s excluding the bench legs). The kitchen previously could only seat two people comfortably, and now the bench can seat up to three with room for additional seating for up to five people around the table. I plan to extend the kitchen table to be the same length as the bench and possibly add a painting or feature on the back wall.
